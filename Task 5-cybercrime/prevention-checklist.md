Stay Safe Online – Checklist

● Make strong passwords that are unique for each app
use both letters and numbers as well as symbols when creating a password.

● Make sure to use 2FA.
When using your email, social media, or bank accounts you should have 2FA enabled.

● Avoid clicking on links from unknown sources.
Phishing scams often come via email, SMS or WhatsApp: please do not click on any such links.

● Verify any job/internship offers.
Keep in mind that a lot of scammers target students with fake job offers, so always verify with the company’s website to see if any offers are legitimate.

● Be careful when using public Wi-Fi.
You should only use public Wi-Fi for accessing banking apps or UPI only for viewing them.

● Limit the information you post on social media.
Do not post your actual home phone or address, your college ID should not be posted publicly on social media.

● Always download apps from a reputable source.
Only get apps from the Google Play Store, do not download APK files from unofficial websites.

● UPI Safety: Your OTP or pin code should never be disclosed
Your bank or app provider will never ask you for your OTP or UPI pin code.

● UPI Safety: Double check ‘collect request’
Scammers will often ask for a ‘money request’ versus a collect request, ensure that you verify the request prior to confirming.

● Sign out when using shared devices.
It is very important that you sign out before leaving a friend’s house and checking out of a cafe/cyber cafe.
