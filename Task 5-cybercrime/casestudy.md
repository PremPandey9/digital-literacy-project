UPI/Online Payment Fraud: A Case Study

Ramesh Gupta, who is 54 years old and is a retired teacher that resides in Bhopal, didn't think much of his cell phone. It was just a tool he normally used to keep in touch with his kids and make the odd payment for his electricity bill. On this day, an innocent Tuesday morning, he was to become a victim of a crime.

The phone rang, and Ramesh was answered by a caller claiming to be an "SBI customer care executive." As he spoke to Ramesh, he seemed to be calm, professional, and reassuring. "Ramesh, there is an issue with your KYC; if you don't act immediately, your account will be frozen within the next few hours."

The entire scam took place as follows:

After gaining Ramesh's trust, the scammer instructed him to download AnyDesk, an application for remote access I.e., he was instructed to download the application for verification purposes.

The scammer used the AnyDesk program and was able to gain complete control of Ramesh's computer.

After Ramesh downloaded the application and granted access to the scammer, he was instructed to open Google Pay and was deceived into authorising a payment to 'verify' a payment of Rs. 1.

With the scammer having access to the computer, he legislatively authorised the payment of Rs. 1 to a fake account (in eight minutes, the scammer took Rs. 47,000 from Ramesh's account by transferring funds to three separate accounts).
